AI Engineer

Full Time

Antwerp

At Brieflee, we're applying AI to one of the most complex and high-stakes domains: law. We're building intelligent systems that help lawyers research, reason, and work with confidence — grounded in real legal data, not guesswork.

Role description

As an AI Engineer at Brieflee, you'll design and build the AI systems that power our legal research and workflow platform. You'll work across the full AI stack — from data pipelines and retrieval systems to LLM integration and evaluation — all applied to the legal domain.

Brieflee is building AI infrastructure for law: retrieval-augmented generation over structured legal data, legal reasoning systems, and intelligent workflows that reflect how lawyers actually work. This role is central to making that happen.

You'll work closely with founders, legal engineers, and product designers to build AI that is accurate, explainable, and trustworthy. Legal professionals need to rely on what we build — hallucinations and vague outputs are not an option.

This is a hands-on engineering role with real ownership. You won't just fine-tune prompts — you'll architect systems, build evaluation frameworks, and shape the core AI capabilities of the product.

If you're excited about applying AI to a domain where precision and trust matter deeply, this role is for you.

What you'll do

As an AI Engineer, you'll own the AI and machine learning layer of Brieflee.

You will:

  • Design and build RAG (retrieval-augmented generation) pipelines over structured legal data — case law, statutes, legal doctrine
  • Integrate and optimize LLM-based systems for legal research, summarization, and analysis
  • Build evaluation and testing frameworks to measure accuracy, relevance, and legal correctness of AI outputs
  • Develop data pipelines for ingesting, cleaning, and structuring large volumes of legal documents
  • Implement search and retrieval systems (vector search, hybrid search, re-ranking) tailored to legal content
  • Collaborate with legal engineers to encode domain knowledge into AI systems
  • Experiment with and deploy new techniques — fine-tuning, chain-of-thought reasoning, structured output generation
  • Monitor and improve system performance, latency, and reliability in production
  • Contribute to architectural decisions about our AI infrastructure and tooling
What we're looking for

We're looking for a strong, pragmatic AI Engineer — someone who can build robust systems and iterate quickly in a fast-moving environment.

You should have:

  • Solid experience in ML/AI engineering (2+ years building production AI systems)
  • Strong proficiency in Python and familiarity with the modern AI/ML stack
  • Hands-on experience with LLMs (GPT, Claude, open-source models) and RAG architectures
  • Experience with vector databases, embeddings, and search/retrieval systems
  • Understanding of NLP fundamentals — text processing, information extraction, classification
  • Ability to design experiments, build evaluations, and reason about AI system quality
  • Comfort working in a startup environment with ambiguity and rapid iteration

Nice to have (but not required):

  • Experience working with legal data or other structured, domain-specific content
  • Familiarity with knowledge graphs, ontologies, or structured data extraction
  • Experience with fine-tuning LLMs or building custom model pipelines
  • Exposure to civil law systems or legal tech products
  • Background in information retrieval or search engineering
How you'll work
  • High autonomy, high responsibility
  • Direct collaboration with founders and core engineering team
  • Strong influence over product direction and AI architecture
  • Focus on substance, not bureaucracy
  • Remote-friendly, outcome-driven culture
Why this role matters

Most legal AI tools produce generic, unreliable outputs that lawyers can't trust. At Brieflee, we're building something different — AI that is grounded in real legal data, structured for precision, and designed for professional use.

This role exists because we believe the quality of AI in legal tech depends on the quality of the engineering behind it. If you want to build AI systems that professionals actually rely on, this is your opportunity.
