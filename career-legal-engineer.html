Role description

As a Senior Legal Engineer at Brieflee, you'll sit at the intersection of law, product, and engineering. Your role is to translate legal complexity into structured, usable, and scalable systems that power our AI-driven legal research and workflow platform.

Brieflee is building the next generation of legal infrastructure: structured case law, connected articles of law, explainable AI outputs, and workflows that actually reflect how lawyers think and work. This role is critical to that mission.

You'll work closely with founders, engineers, and product designers to shape how legal knowledge is modeled, extracted, validated, and used across the platform. You'll help define how legal documents are structured, how legal reasoning is represented, and how AI outputs remain accurate, traceable, and defensible.

This is a senior role with real ownership. You won't just "review outputs" — you'll design systems, define legal logic, and influence core product decisions.

If you enjoy bridging law and technology, care deeply about precision, and want to shape how legal professionals work in the age of AI, this role is for you.

What you'll do

As a Senior Legal Engineer, you'll take ownership of the legal intelligence layer of Brieflee.

You will:

  • Design and maintain structured representations of legal knowledge (case law, holdings, legal issues, citations, articles of law)
  • Translate legal reasoning into system logic usable by engineers and AI models
  • Define and refine legal taxonomies, ontologies, and metadata schemas
  • Work closely with engineers to guide how legal data is ingested, structured, and queried
  • Review and validate AI-generated legal outputs for accuracy, relevance, and traceability
  • Help define workflows for legal research, drafting, and analysis that match real legal practice
  • Contribute to product decisions where legal correctness and usability matter
  • Identify edge cases, risks, and failure modes in AI-assisted legal reasoning
  • Act as an internal legal authority on how the product should behave in real-world legal contexts
What we're looking for

We're looking for a senior, sharp, and opinionated Legal Engineer — someone who can think independently and challenge assumptions.

You should have:

  • A strong legal background (law degree required; bar admission is a plus but not mandatory)
  • Several years of experience working with complex legal materials (case law, statutes, legal analysis)
  • Demonstrated interest or experience in legal tech, legal engineering, or applied AI in law
  • The ability to translate legal concepts into structured, system-friendly logic
  • Comfort working closely with engineers and product teams
  • Strong written and analytical skills — precision matters here
  • A pragmatic mindset: you care about what works, not academic perfection

Nice to have (but not required):

  • Experience with legal research platforms, knowledge graphs, or document automation
  • Familiarity with AI/LLMs, prompt design, or evaluation of AI outputs
  • Exposure to civil law systems (Belgium / EU law is a plus)
  • Experience in a startup or fast-moving product environment
How you'll work
  • High autonomy, high responsibility
  • Direct collaboration with founders and core engineering team
  • Strong influence over product direction and legal architecture
  • Focus on substance, not bureaucracy
  • Remote-friendly, outcome-driven culture
Why this role matters

Legal AI fails when legal logic is an afterthought. At Brieflee, it's the foundation. This role exists because we believe legal structure, correctness, and explainability are non-negotiable — and that requires senior legal minds embedded directly into product and engineering.

If you want to help define how legal professionals will work in the next decade, this is your seat at the table.
